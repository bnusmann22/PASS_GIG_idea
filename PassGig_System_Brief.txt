01 OVERVIEW
What is PassGig?
PassGig is an intelligent economic platform that connects unemployed Nigerian youth with gig
opportunities posted by informal traders and artisans — while simultaneously building a financial
identity for both sides that unlocks access to credit, savings, and insurance. It is built specifically
for Nigeria's informal economy.
The Core Problem We Are Solving
Nigeria has over 13 million unemployed youth and a sprawling informal economy of traders, artisans,
mechanics, tailors, food vendors, and repair workers. These two groups exist in the same
neighborhoods but are disconnected. Traders cannot find reliable help. Youth cannot prove their skills
or get paid reliably. Neither group has the financial history needed to access formal credit or
insurance — because the formal system was never built for them.
PassGig fixes the connection layer and, in doing so, generates the transaction and behavioral data
that builds financial identities from scratch.

03 HOW THE SYSTEM WORKS
The Three Layers of PassGig
The platform is built in three interconnected layers. Each layer feeds the next, creating a
compounding system that gets more valuable as more people use it.
Layer 1 — Onboarding & Economic Passport
Every user — youth or trader — creates an Economic Passport when they join PassGig. This is a
dynamic digital profile that starts with declared information and grows richer over time through real
activity on the platform.
For Youth:
    Full name, age, location (LGA level)
    Skills declared: e.g. Graphic Design, Plumbing, Delivery, Tailoring, Carpentry
    Availability: days/hours per week
    Portfolio upload: photos, certificates, references (optional at start)
    Initial trust score generated by AI from declared data and phone verification
For Traders / Artisans:
    Business name, trade type, location
    Nature of tasks they typically need help with
    How often they hire, rough budget range
    Initial trust score from business verification signals
The passport is not static. Every completed gig, every payment made on time, every positive
rating received — all of it updates the passport score in real time. The passport becomes a living
financial identity, not just a one-time registration.
Layer 2 — The Gig Matching Engine
Traders post gigs on the platform. The AI matching engine then finds the best-fit youth workers based
on multiple signals and ranks them for the trader to review and hire.
Matching Signal
What It Does
Skill alignment
Compares the gig requirements to youth declared skills

Once a match is confirmed, the trader deposits payment into escrow via Squad API. The youth
worker is notified, accepts the gig, completes the work, and the trader releases payment. Both sides
rate each other. Both passports update.

04 SQUAD API INTEGRATION
How Squad Powers the Core of PassGig
Squad API is not a token integration added at the end. It is structurally embedded into three critical
functions of the platform:
Squad API Function
How PassGig Uses It
Why It Matters
Payment Escrow
Trader deposits gig payment upfront into escrow. Funds are held until gig is marked complete.
Protects both parties. Youth knows payment is guaranteed. Trader knows work must be delivered.
Worker Disbursement
On gig completion and trader confirmation, Squad releases funds directly to worker's account or wallet.
Fast, reliable payment to workers — removes the 'I'll pay you later' problem common in informal hiring.
Transaction Data Feed
All payments flow through Squad, generating a structured transaction history per user.
This transaction history is the raw data that powers the AI credit scoring model for both youth and traders.
Business Payments
Traders can use Squad to receive payments from their own customers, logged to their PassGig profile.
Adds a broader revenue signal to the trader's economic passport beyond just hiring activity.
Disqualification note from the brief: A solution with no meaningful Squad API integration will
not be eligible for top placements regardless of scores in other criteria. PassGig's escrow and
disbursement architecture ensures Squad is central — not cosmetic.
05 AI & DATA INTELLIGENCE LAYER
What the AI Actually Does
The AI layer has three distinct, concrete jobs in PassGig. Each one is technically demonstrable at the
hackathon:
1. Trust Scoring Engine
Generates an initial passport score at onboarding using declared data, phone verification, BVN
signals (optional), and device signals. Updates the score dynamically after every completed gig,
payment event, and rating. This is a classification/regression model trained on behavioral and
transactional signals — similar to alternative credit scoring used by fintechs like Fairmoney and
Carbon, adapted for the informal economy context.
2. Gig Matching Algorithm
Ranks available workers for each posted gig using a weighted scoring function across skill fit,
location, passport score, availability, and category experience. Can be implemented as a
collaborative filtering model or a simpler weighted ranking system depending on data volume at demo
stage. Outputs a ranked shortlist for the trader.
3. Skill Gap & Demand Recommender
Analyzes gig posting patterns across locations and categories to identify unmet demand. For
example: if there are 40 gig postings for electricians in Surulere but only 5 electricians registered, the
system flags this and recommends nearby youth workers to consider upskilling. This is the system
'learning and improving over time' the challenge brief specifically asks for.

09 RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS FOR THE TEAM
What to Do Now
1. Align on scope for the demo
Decide: are you showing both the youth flow AND the trader flow, or focusing on one? Both is ideal
for impact, but one working end-to-end is better than two broken ones.
2. Set up Squad API sandbox
Get API keys and test the escrow and payment flow immediately. This takes the most integration time
and is the highest-weighted judging criteria.
3. Build the passport & matching MVP first
Onboarding form → AI score → gig posting → shortlist display → escrow payment. That is your demo
spine.
4. Assign the AI model early
Even a simple weighted scoring function qualifies. You need someone on the team focused purely on
the trust scoring and matching logic from day one.
5. Talk to at least 3 real users
The rubric explicitly asks: have you spoken to your target users? One unemployed youth, one market
trader, one artisan. Even WhatsApp voice notes count as evidence.
6. Prepare the one-pager
The judges keep this after your presentation. It should be: problem, solution, Squad APIs used, four
pillars addressed, impact numbers. One A4 page. Design it properly.
Remember: A working demo beats a perfect slide deck. Build the core loop — user signs up →
gig posted → match made → Squad payment flows → passports update — and make sure it runs
live without breaking. That is your winning condition.
