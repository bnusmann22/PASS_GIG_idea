PassGig UX story — two user journeys: Emeka the youth job seeker and Mama Ngozi the trader, showing how they connect through the PassGig platform

UX story — two journeys, one platform
Meet the users
EO

Emeka, 23

Surulere, Lagos

Graduated one year ago. No job yet. Can do graphic design, delivery runs, and basic electrical work — but has no way to prove it or get paid reliably.

MN

Mama Ngozi, 41

Balogun Market, Lagos

Runs a busy fabric shop. Needs reliable delivery help 3× a week and an assistant for busy market days. Has steady cash flow but no formal credit history.

Emeka's journey — getting found

Downloads PassGig, signs up

Emeka finds PassGig through a WhatsApp group. He signs up with his phone number and BVN (optional). Takes 3 minutes.

Onboarding screen

Builds his Economic Passport

He selects his skills from a visual category grid: Delivery, Graphic Design, Electrical. Sets his availability — Mon to Sat, mornings. Uploads one reference photo of past work.

Passport builder screen

AI generates his initial trust score

The system scores him on declared data, phone verification, and location signals. No payslip or CV needed.

Mama Ngozi's journey — finding reliable help

Signs up as a trader

Mama Ngozi registers her business — shop name, trade type (fabric retail), location. She takes a photo of her store front as verification.

Trader onboarding screen

Posts her first gig

She fills a simple form: task type → Delivery. Date → tomorrow. Hours → 8am to 12pm. Pay → ₦2,500. Notes → "deliver fabric bundles to 4 addresses in Yaba." Done in under 2 minutes.

Post a gig screen

Sees her matched shortlist

The AI returns 5 workers ranked by skill match, proximity, score, and availability. Emeka is #2. She sees his skills, his score, his distance (4km), and 0 past gigs. She picks him anyway — his score is decent and he's closest.

Match shortlist screen
AI ranked Emeka #2 based on: delivery skill match ✓ · location proximity 4km ✓ · available tomorrow 8am ✓ · passport score 38.

Pays into escrow via Squad

She confirms Emeka and is prompted to secure the gig payment. She pays ₦2,500 into Squad escrow via her bank transfer or Squad wallet. Emeka sees "Payment secured — gig confirmed."

Payment screen (Squad API)
The crossover — gig day
What happens on the day

Emeka shows up at Mama Ngozi's shop at 8am. He makes all 4 deliveries. At 12pm, Mama Ngozi opens the app, reviews the gig, and marks it complete. Squad releases ₦2,500 directly to Emeka's wallet — instantly.

Both sides rate each other

Mama Ngozi gives Emeka 5 stars — punctual, careful with goods. Emeka rates the gig 4 stars — clear instructions, paid fast. Both ratings are submitted.

Rating screen

Both passports update automatically

The system processes the completed gig, payment event, and ratings. Both profiles improve.

Emeka unlocks his first credit offer

After 18 gigs and a score of 82, Emeka gets a notification — "You're eligible for a ₦15,000 credit line." He applies in-app. No salary slip, no guarantor. His gig history is his proof.

Financial unlocks screen

Mama Ngozi qualifies for a business loan referral

After 3 months of consistent Squad payments and hiring activity, her trader passport score is 71. The platform surfaces a working capital loan offer from a partner fintech — ₦150,000 at 4% monthly. The first loan she has ever been offered.

Trader financial profile screen

Mama Ngozi hires Emeka again — and again

She saves Emeka as a "preferred worker." Now she can re-hire him with one tap, skipping the matching step entirely. The platform suggests she set up a recurring gig arrangement.

The AI recommends Emeka consider upskilling in tailoring — there are 23 unfilled tailoring gigs near Surulere this week.
